AI KTU Notes App

A Smart Educational Ecosystem for KTU Students

1. Abstract

The AI KTU Notes App is a modern educational platform built for students under the APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University (KTU). By integrating Google's Gemini Large Language Model, the application moves beyond traditional static document storage. It provides a suite of intelligent tools including RAG-based chatbots, auto-summarizers, and visual generators that facilitate active learning and deep conceptual understanding.

2. Introduction

Students today have access to vast amounts of digital study material, yet the efficiency of learning hasn't kept pace. Most platforms act as simple file repositories. This project aims to bridge the gap between "having the notes" and "understanding the context" by using AI agents that can read, interpret, and explain complex academic content in real-time.

3. Objectives

  • Centralized Access: Provide a unified portal for all KTU branch and semester notes.
  • Cognitive Offloading: Use AI Summarization to reduce the time spent reading irrelevant sections.
  • Interactive Tutoring: Deploy a 24/7 AI Chatbot for instant clarification of doubts.
  • Visual Synthesis: Automatically convert textual processes into interactive diagrams.
  • Knowledge Assessment: Enable auto-generation of quizzes for self-evaluation.

4. Existing Systems

Current platforms used by KTU students primarily include Telegram bots (like KTU Assist), static websites (Keralanotes, KTU Management), and shared Google Drives. While these provide the raw data, they share several common drawbacks:

  • Manual Search: Students must dig through hierarchies to find a specific topic.
  • No Interactivity: Static PDFs cannot answer follow-up questions or adapt to the user's pace.
  • Passive Learning: Reading through 100+ pages of notes often leads to cognitive fatigue without active recall tools.
  • Inconsistent Quality: Materials are often unorganized or lack clear summaries.

5. Literature Survey

"The integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) in EdTech has transitioned from simple text generation to Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), which allows AI to provide factual, context-specific answers from a trusted knowledge base." (Modern EdTech Trends, 2025)

Surveys of current educational tools highlight that students prefer modular, bite-sized content over long-form text. Platforms like Notion AI and Otter.ai have shown that AI-summarized transcription significantly improves retention. In the specific context of KTU, which follows a rigorous engineering syllabus, the need for "conceptual visualization" and "logical code explanations" is paramount.

6. Problem Statement

KTU students face a high-density curriculum where traditional study methods are often time-consuming. There is a lack of a "smart" companion that can distill complex engineering concepts into simple explanations, generate visual aids on-demand, and provide a branch-specific filtered experience. The existing fragmentation of resources leads to significant time loss during exam preparation.

7. Overall Architecture

The system is built on a distributed cloud architecture. The Flutter frontend communicates with Firebase for user data and storage, while a specialized AI Backend handles the heavy lifting of document processing and LLM interaction.

8. Module Description

  • Authentication Module: Handles secure login and signup using Firebase, with branch and semester profiling.
  • Library Module: A hierarchical system to organize notes by Semester -> Subject -> Module.
  • AI Summarization Engine: Uses NLP to extract core concepts and present them in three granularity levels.
  • RAG Doubt Solver: Combines the prompt with the PDF's text embeddings to ensure AI answers are strictly based on the provided notes.
  • Diagram Visualizer: Maps textual logic to Mermaid.js code, rendered via a mobile WebView.
  • Community & Chat: Real-time peer-to-peer messaging for collaborative learning.
